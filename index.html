Customer Churn Prediction & Revenue Recovery Engine

IBM Telco Dataset · 7,043 Customers

Most churn models are built to impress data scientists. This one was built to sit in front of a CFO — going beyond accuracy to answer who is about to leave, when, and what it costs to keep them.

What's inside:

  • Exploratory Analysis: Churn rate, revenue at risk, and loyalty curves by contract type, service tier, and payment method.
  • K-Means Segmentation: 4 customer personas, each with its own churn trajectory and recommended intervention strategy.
  • 3-Model Comparison: Logistic Regression vs Random Forest vs Gradient Boosting — evaluated on recall, not accuracy.
  • Revenue Quantification: Predictions converted into ₹ across 3 retention scenarios with full ROI modelling.
  • Kaplan-Meier Survival Analysis: Implemented from first principles with log-rank significance testing by contract type.
  • Power BI Executive Dashboard: Framed for a business audience, not a data team.

Key results:

  • 26.5% baseline churn rate — ₹4.25 lakh/month in simulated revenue at risk.
  • 85.2% recall on the churned class — Gradient Boosting at optimised threshold 0.38.
  • Month 10 identified as the pre-emptive intervention window via survival analysis.
  • ₹8.69 lakh/year recoverable under the moderate retention scenario (~520% ROI).
  • Top 3 churn drivers: tenure, contract type, internet service tier.

Survival analysis showed customers exit in a concentrated window between Month 12 and 18 — a retention campaign at Month 18 is already too late. Month 10 turned this from a prediction model into an operational calendar.

Tech Stack: Python, Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-learn, Lifelines, SQL, Power BI, Matplotlib, Plotly

Swiggy Hyperlocal Success Predictor

8,000+ Restaurants Analysed

Most restaurant owners believe "lower prices = more orders." This analysis proves the opposite: the food delivery market is quality-elastic but price-inelastic — a 1-star rating increase is 36x more impactful for success than a ₹100 price cut.

What's inside:

  • AI Segmentation: TF-IDF and K-Means clustering identified 5 distinct "Business Personas," from Heritage Staples to the Sweet Tooth Niche.
  • Predictive Modeling: A regression framework (R² = 0.40) quantifying the drivers of restaurant success.
  • Gap Analysis: Identified high-demand, low-quality localities — a "gold mine" for high-quality new entrants (e.g. Chembur, Pitam Pura).
  • Weather Intelligence: Quantified the "Rain Lift" effect — a significant surge in comfort food demand during monsoons.

This isn't just a coding exercise — it's a site-selection tool and a strategic playbook for ROI, built entirely on Geospatial AI.

Tech Stack: Python, Pandas, Scikit-learn, TF-IDF, K-Means, Regression, Plotly, Power BI, Geospatial Analytics
